Questions for All Adopters:

Do you have other pets and what would their possible reaction be to the new pet?

How would your social life and work affect your ability to care for the pet?

Do you have a plan in place for your pet during vacations and/or work related travels?

How do the people you live with feel about adopting a pet?

Do any of your family/ household members have any health condition that may be affected by the presence of a pet?

Is there an adult in the household that has agreed to take the responsibility of caring for the pet?

Time Considerations:

Pets need to be fed twice or thrice a day, more often for puppies. They also need a regular supply of fresh water.

As a responsible pet parent we recommend you to spend at least one hour of your day with your pet. Give them your direct attention by either exercising, grooming and /or playing with them.

If your pet has an abundance of energy, he/she will need more exercise and interactive toys to keep them busy and entertained.

Pets with a long coat of hair required at least 20 minutes of grooming each day.

Pets with medical conditions require more attention to cater to their special needs, such as timely injections for pets suffering from diabetes.

Adopted pets usually require additional time for bonding and reassurance during the early weeks. However, this may not always be the case.
